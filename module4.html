Reflective Piece

The Software Engineering Project Management module aims to provide students with an in-depth understanding of the basic principles of Project Management and software engineering principles, recognizing their relationship and interactions with other computing and design disciplines. We had a chance to dive deeper into software development life cycles, examine the importance of managing a project, and study a project manager's activities at each stage of the SDLC.

We initiated the design proposal early after the second week with a comparative table illustrating the merits and drawbacks of applying the following models: Waterfall, V-Model, Spiral, and Agile. I found myself to be most productive during the time we collaborated to produce the design proposal. One teammate and I were responsible for creating the document's outline and combining the contribution from others for the final submission as we agreed to take the role of the technical writer during this process. I also initiated a Jira board, and the team started segmenting the project into epics, user stories, and tasks. The team was struggling at first, trying to understand the concepts behind those components and learning how to organize everything in a logical and structured way.

Gathering requirement is one of the crucial engagement activities of any project planning. Communication and negotiation skills play an essential role during this phase. On the one hand, we can accept up to ten requirements to put into the design proposals as a development team. The top five of those with the highest priority will be implemented for the demonstration. On the other hand, as a customer team, we need to get as many requirements as possible accepted by the developer team. Getting different points of view from both teams helped us to understand the requirements and what we needed to achieve clearly.

During the first few weeks preparing for the mid-module assignment, I joined a collaborative discussion on the most common reasons for a project failure. My initial post indicated that poor project planning and execution, communication, and quality control are the leading causes, along with the examples of IBM System 360, EDS-BSkyB CRM, and European Space Agency Ariane 5 to support those points. Project management methodologies and techniques were also discussed to see how they would help to remove the obstacles and support team collaboration. Learning from other threads within the same topics, it is evident that a project may fail due to various reasons, objectively or subjectively. However, it is complicated to determine the real reason for a project failure. All the actors part of a project are interrelated and depend on each other. The development and deployment of software systems is a multidimensional process where people and technology are interconnected.

The objectives of any software are to provide the most efficient tool for the job. The software itself is supposed to be easy to use, help promote the brand and engage with the customers. In my professional career, I have co-conducted a few customers engagement workshops where we tried to figure out what the customers need. Some activities that can be mentioned are: analyzing the user data to extract the actual pain points that cause users problems, creating detailed personas to describe whom we are building products for, ideation or brain-storming for getting input and agreement from the whole team, working with scenarios and storyboards to describe how we expect users to complete their tasks and see how the interaction between users and the system happens. This module tasked us to examine another aspect of user experience management: human emotions. Studying the CUR model by Minge & Thuring (2018) changed my experience research mindset. From a developer's point of view, I focused on the instrumental qualities, which are the functionalities of the software itself. However, non-instrumental qualities are essential factors influencing customers' purchase decisions, not to mention other aspects such as personality, lifestyle, and culture.

Throughout the modules, there were several other formative activities to explore the wide range of techniques to measure and monitor the software development process, such as estimating, planning, risk assessment, and quality control. In my opinion, the accuracy of these metrics heavily depends on the project manager's experience and the development team.

I would admit that the most challenging part of this module is to figure out how to collaborate effectively with everyone. Because of the time-zone difference, I can rarely attend live seminars and team meetings. Thus, I must spend extra time catching up with the team using the meeting notes where all team discussions and actionable items are located.
